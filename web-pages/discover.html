Discover

Mammals

Whale

As large marine mammals, undertake extensive migrations and display complex communication through vocalizations, while also exhibiting behaviors like breaching.

Elephant

The largest land mammals, showcase intelligence and social structures within family groups led by a matriarch, utilizing their trunks for tasks like feeding and communication.

Hippopotamus

Despite their large size, are agile and territorial animals, often displaying aggressiveness, especially in defending their water habitats.

Polar Bear

Adapted to cold environments, polar bears excel in swimming and primarily feed on seals. They lead solitary lives, except during mating or when a female is accompanied by cubs.

Zebra

Recognized for their black and white striped coats, are social herbivores that form groups for protection and utilize their stripes as a form of camouflage.

Tiger

Large carnivorous cats, exhibit solitary hunting behaviors, primarily during the night, and are known for their strength, agility, and powerful roars.

Fish

Clownfish

Clownfish exhibit unique social structures, including a dominant female and the ability to change sex.

Betta Fish

Betta fish, also known as Siamese fighting fish, are territorial and renowned for their vibrant colors and flowing fins.

Shark

Sharks are apex predators with a keen sense of smell and electroreception.

Moorish Idol

Moorish idols have distinctive appearances with long, trailing dorsal fins and are challenging to keep in captivity due to specific dietary requirements.

Lionfish

Lionfish are venomous predators with elaborate fin displays.

Tuna

Tuna, as highly migratory and warm-blooded fish, boast streamlined bodies built for fast swimming.

Birds

Peacock

The peacock's unique courtship display involves fanning vibrant tail feathers with distinctive eye patterns, serving both aesthetic and defensive purposes.

Bald Eagle

Known for remarkable eyesight, bald eagles engage in aerial acrobatics during courtship, showcasing their prowess in the sky.

Atlantic Puffin

Atlantic puffins form strong pair bonds, engaging in unique courtship behaviors with brightly colored bills, and are skilled underwater hunters.

Hummingbird

Hummingbirds exhibit unparalleled agility, hovering with rapid wing flaps and, in some cases, flying backward for precise navigation during nectar feeding.

Northern Cardinal

Male northern cardinals stand out with vivid red plumage, displaying territorial behavior and occasionally attacking their reflections during the breeding season.

Secretary Bird

Secretary birds use powerful legs for unique prey-stomping hunting techniques, and their courtship involves high aerial circling with synchronized wing displays and calls.

Reptiles

Green Sea Turtle

Known for their migratory behavior across vast oceanic distances, their herbivorous diet, and the unique pattern of green fat underneath their carapace, which gives them their name.

Jackson's Chameleon

Characterized by their three distinct horns (nasal and supraorbital), their ability to change color for communication and temperature regulation, and their distinctive, triocular vision.

King Cobra

Distinguished by its impressive size, potent neurotoxic venom, and its unique ability to rear up and form a hood, making it one of the most iconic and feared snake species.

Anaconda

Particularly the green anaconda, are the heaviest and one of the longest snakes globally, known for their powerful constricting abilities and semi-aquatic lifestyle.

Leopard Gecko

The Leopard Gecko is recognized for its ability to shed its tail as a defense mechanism, a process known as autotomy, and its unique vocalizations, which are unusual among gecko species.

Galápagos Tortoise

Known for their exceptional longevity, with some individuals living well over a century, and their iconic shells that vary in shape and size between different islands of the Galápagos archipelago.

Amphibians

Poison Dart Frog

Known for their bright and vibrant colors, these small frogs are native to Central and South America, and some species possess potent toxins.

Red-Eyed Tree Frog

Recognized for its striking appearance, with vibrant red eyes and colorful markings, found in Central America.

Salamander

A diverse family of amphibians known for their often sleek bodies and various adaptations, such as the ability to regenerate lost body parts.

Horned Frog

Also known as pacman frogs, they are characterized by their large mouths and are ambush predators found in South America.

Fire Salamander

Known for its contrasting black and yellow coloration, this species is found in various habitats across Europe.

Mossy Frog

Hailing from Vietnam, this unique tree frog exhibits remarkable camouflage, resembling moss-covered surfaces.
